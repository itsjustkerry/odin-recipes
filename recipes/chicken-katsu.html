Chicken Katsu

Description

Katsu is a Japanese dish of crispy fried cutlets coated with Panko bread crumbs. Popular varieties are chicken katsu (like this recipe) and tonkatsu (which is made with pork).

Chicken katsu is Japanese-style fried chicken. It is served with white rice and tonkats sauce

This recipe serves four people.

Ingredients

  • 4 skinless, boneless chicken breast halves.
  • Salt and pepper to taste.
  • 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour.
  • 1 Egg.
  • 1 cup of panko bread crumbs.
  • 1 cup of either canola or vegetable oil.

Steps

  1. Season chicken breasts on both sides with salt and pepper.
  2. Place flour, beaten egg, and panko crumbs into separate shallow dishes.
  3. Coat chicken breasts in flour, shaking off any excess; dip into egg, and then press into panko crumbs until well coated on both sides.
  4. Place chicken in the hot oil, and fry until golden brown, 3 or 4 minutes per side.
  5. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
  6. Serve with tonkatsu sauce or miso soup.

Enjoy!!
